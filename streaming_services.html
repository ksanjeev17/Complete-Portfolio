Out of the many streaming services out there, there are four that stand apart as the most used platforms: Disney+, Hulu, Netflix, and Amazon Prime. While there are other services like Peacock and Paramount+, none of them have the bandwidth to provide MCU movies to your individual screens. Now here are those top four platforms and what Marvel features are on each.

Amazon Prime

Amazon Prime has nothing available that is free. It does, however, have everything ready to purchase.

Disney+

Disney + is the home for everything Marvel related. The service includes all of Phase 1, Phase 2, Phase 3, and Phase 4 except for Spider-Man: Homecoming, Far From Home, and No Way Home. Disney+ does not have the rights to the Spider-Man movies as it is owned by Sony. It is also the only platform that includes the Marvel TV Shows as well.

Hulu

Hulu includes a very random list of Marvel content that is constantly changing. Current highlights are Spider-Man: Homecoming + Far From Home, Ant Man & the Wasp, Deadpool: 1 + 2, Black Panther, Iron Man 3, Thor: The Dark World, and Venom.

Netflix

Netflix currently has no Marvel movies available in the U.S. The platform does include Spider-Man: Into The Spiderverse which is a non-main cannon movie and stands alone apart from the Spider-Man franchise.
